# SEO strategy in the answer-engine era (evidence base, as of 2026-06)

> Snapshot of a fast-moving system. Every CTR/AIO percentage below is a
> 2025–early-2026 figure — treat as **directional, not a fixed constant**, and
> re-verify periodically. Sourced from a 26-source, adversarially-verified deep
> research pass (21 claims confirmed, 4 popular claims killed — see "Debunked").

## The shift
Search moved from "ten blue links" to an **answer-engine model**: the SERP (and
ChatGPT/Perplexity/Gemini) increasingly answers the query in place. The job is no
longer "rank #1 and harvest the click" on informational terms — it's **be the
source the answer is built from, and win the queries that still produce clicks**
(commercial / planning / long-tail / branded intent).

## Verified findings
1. **AI Overviews are structural and growing** — ~13% of queries (up from 6.5% in
   early 2025). Where one appears, organic clicks roughly halve: 8% click-rate
   with an AI summary vs 15% without (Pew, 68,879 searches); Seer measured −61%
   organic CTR on informational AIO queries.
2. **AI citation = brand impression, not traffic** — users click a link *inside*
   an AI summary ~1% of the time; sessions end with no click more often (26% vs
   16%). Model AEO/GEO citation as reach, never as a click source.
3. **Outcome ranking for a query**: *no-AIO query (3.35% CTR) > cited under AIO
   (2.07%) > uncited under AIO (0.94%)*. So: prefer intents that don't trigger an
   AIO; if one triggers, being cited still beats being uncited.
4. **What makes AI engines cite you: topical relevance + organic ranking
   position** (controlled 252k-trial RAG study). Earning a high organic rank and
   covering the topic genuinely is the GEO strategy.
5. **Core-update drops are not penalties** and recover slowly (3–6+ months, often
   only on a later update). Build durable quality; don't panic-rewrite on a dip.
   (Distinct from SPAM updates, which *do* target scaled-content abuse & link
   schemes.)
6. **Zero-click is the majority (~60%)** but predates AIO and isn't solely caused
   by it — assume declining click yield on informational head terms regardless.
7. **Travel incumbents are retreating from SEO** — Tripadvisor (Feb 2026 earnings)
   blamed AI Overviews for traffic decline and projects SEO to <10% of
   Experiences booking volume by end-2026. Warning *and* opening for small,
   genuinely-expert sites.

## Durable principles → bias every recommendation toward these
- **First-hand experience + information gain** — the one thing a generic AI
  summary can't reproduce. This is the moat.
- **Topical authority** via deep clusters, not thin one-off pages.
- **Match search intent**; target where clicks still exist (commercial/long-tail).
- **Brand signals + UGC presence** (Reddit, the answer engines now mediate
  discovery).
- **Technical hygiene + earn ranking** (ranking feeds AI-citation odds).
- **Measure leading indicators** (GSC impressions/positions/distinct-queries,
  branded volume) and **business outcomes** (GA4 engaged sessions/conversions) —
  not raw organic clicks, which decline structurally.

## Debunked — do NOT build strategy on these (killed in verification)
- ❌ Formatting/structure tricks get you cited by AI (no measurable effect).
- ❌ "Cited sites get +35% organic / +91% paid CTR."
- ❌ Four binary "gatekeeper" factors decide citation.
- ❌ Generic GEO/AEO formatting checklists (FAQ schema, bullet-everything,
  Q&A-ify) as a citation lever — weakly evidenced.

## Avoid (risk)
- Mass AI-generated / programmatic pages → March 2026 scaled-content-abuse risk,
  and exactly what AIOs out-compete.
- Treating AIO citations as a traffic line item.
- Head-to-head on generic head terms against Booking/Tripadvisor/Reddit.

## Honest caveats / where experts disagree
- CTR-magnitude studies (Seer, Semrush, thedigitalbloom) are SEO-vendor research
  that disclaim causation; cited-vs-not gaps are partly self-selection. Strongest
  least-conflicted evidence: Pew (independent, behavioral) + Google's own
  core-update guidance.
- Causal disagreement is real: Pew/Seer show a *large* click drop; Semrush's
  same-keyword panel shows zero-click barely moved. They measure different
  metrics — don't average them.
- **Unverified in this pass (treat as principle, not 2026 fact):** whether
  `llms.txt` affects AI citation (likely speculative); current independent weight
  of Core Web Vitals / schema; specific link-building tactics; the exact
  programmatic-content spam threshold.
